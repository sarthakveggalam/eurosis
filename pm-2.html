Data Processing for M&E

Successful Implementation of a M&E Database in two Local Organizations

Under the LCD two local organizations benefited from technical assistance to implement a data processing for M&A: Organization A and Organization B. Organization A is a local organization advocating for better primary education in North of Mozambique. Organization A helps school management committees to improve the school management and the primary education quality. Organization B mobilizes the religious communities established in Mozambique in particular, and society in general, to develop activities for the eradication of malaria in Mozambique.

This story (problem, intervention and results) relates briefly the development and implementation of a data Processing Platform for monitoring and evaluation of these 2 Organizations interventions in Mozambique.

The problem

Organization A M&E data was not structurally designed and stored as a data base. Data was stored using a “general table”. Cells were merged, blank cells appeared in the middle of tables, etc.

Thus, information could not be easily and efficiently produced in the form of charts and/or tables. Instead of recording the data for each implementation period, the M&E staff would calculate accumulated data in a separate hand calculator and replace the last period accumulated data with new data.

Thus, it was not possible to do times series analyses. Organization B records their activities in 3 different data base. The data are entered in province level.

Therefore, quarterly the province delegation sends 3 files to the headquarter. In total the headquarter receives 15 database files quarterly. The data consolidation and data processing of 15 files manually is hard work and time consuming.

There was no dashboard or any chart or table to present readable information. Thus, data was recorded, but not adequately analyzed. On Organization B part of the data were not being processed and analyzed.

There was no data to inform project indicators on Organization A. Thus, level of achievement was not analyzed.

The intervention

Through the LCD initiative, EUROSIS’ technical assistance team and Organization A’s staff deeply restructured the data base and created a simple and effective dashboard. The technical assistance team worked with these Organizations to: a) identify the main constraints of the data base in place;

b) define requirements for a proper monitoring and evaluation data base; c) design and develop a data base according to the requirements; d) design and develop a dashboard; e) provide remote support to ensure sustainable use of the new platform.

The results

The data recorded at Organization A data base is now properly converted into information that can be used to a better decision taking related to the improvement of education quality in primary schools at 5 districts in North of Mozambique.

At Organization B the 15 databases files produced quarterly are automatically consolidated and, charts and tables produced for a better data analysis and decision taking.
