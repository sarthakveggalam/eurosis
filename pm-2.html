Data Processing for M&E

M&E Database in a local organization

UATAF is a local organization advocating for better primary education in North of Mozambique. UATAF helps school management committees to improve the school management and the primary education quality. The Organization benefitted from a few interventions under the LCD.

This story (problem, intervention and results) relates briefly the development and implementation of a data base for monitoring and evaluation of UATAF interventions in 5 districts of North Mozambique.

The problem

UATAF’s Monitoring and Evaluation data was not structurally designed and stored as a data base. Data was stored using a “general table”. Cells were merged, blank cells appeared in the middle of tables, etc. Thus, information could not be easily and efficiently produced in the form of charts and/or tables.

Instead of recording the data for each implementation period, the M&E staff would calculate accumulated data in a separate hand calculator and replace the last period accumulated data with new data. Thus, it was not possible to do times series analyses.

There was no dashboard or any chart or table to present readable information. Thus data was recorded, but not adequately analyzed.
There was no data to inform project indicators. Thus, level of achievement was not analyzed.

The intervention

Through the LCD initiative, EUROSIS’ technical assistance team and UATAF’s staff deeply restructured the data base and created a simple and effective dashboard. The technical assistance team worked with UATAF to: a) identify the main constraints of the data base in place; b) define requirements for a proper monitoring and evaluation data base;

c) design and develop a data base according to the requirements; d) recover all the historical data from the paper filed forms; e) record all the 3 years data in the new data base; f) design and develop a dashboard; g) provide remote support to ensure sustainable use of the new platform.

The results

The data recorded at UATAF data base is now properly converted into information that can be used to a better decision taking related to the improvement of education quality in primary schools at 5 districts in North of Mozambique.
